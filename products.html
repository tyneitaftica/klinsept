5. Klinjav (Eau De Javel)

KLINJAV (Sodium hypochlorite) commonly known as bleach, is most frequently used as a disinfecting agent. It is a broad-spectrum disinfectant that is effective for the disinfection of viruses, bacteria, fungi, and mycobacterium.

5 Litres and 1 Litre Packed In Opaque High-Density Plastic Jerricans

MECHANISM OF ACTION (AS A DISINFECTANT)

When it reacts with microbes, sodium hypochlorite attacks proteins in the cells causing the proteins to aggregate and the microbes to clump together and die. It can also cause cell membranes to burst. This broad-spectrum attack makes Sodium Hypochlorite effective against a wide range of bacteria.

OTHER USES OF SODIUM HYPOCHLORITE

As a bleaching agent: Sodium hypochlorite causes a chemical reaction called oxidation when they come into contact with certain stains, certain germs or other organisms, and sometimes clothing dyes. Bleach is a very useful chemical both around the house and for large-scale use. You can use bleach to remove stains on clothing or to whiten your laundry.

To disinfect surfaces: Sodium Hypochlorite is used to disinfect surfaces, too, especially in the kitchen and bathroom. Hospital personnel use Sodium Hypochlorite as a disinfectant, hotels use it to clean and disinfect bed linens and surfaces, and restaurants disinfect food preparation surfaces with Sodium Hypochlorite.

6. Klintol (Chloroxylenol solution, an equivalent to Dettol)

KLINTOL SOLUTION Contains Chloroxylenol 4.8% which is an antimicrobial used to treat cuts, bites, stings, and abrasions, and used as an antiseptic hand cleaner.

Indications

The predominant medical applications for which chloroxylenol is formally indicated for therapeutic use is as an application to the skin for use in cuts, bites, stings and abrasions.

MECHANISM OF ACTION

As a phenol antiseptic, it is believed that the hydroxyl -OH groups of the chloroxylenol molecule bind to certain proteins on the cell membrane of bacteria, and disrupt the membrane so as to allow the contents of the bacterial cell to leak out. This allows chloroxylenol to enter the bacterial cell to bind further with more proteins and enzymes to disable the cell’s functioning. At particularly high concentrations of chloroxylenol, the protein and nucleic acid content of targeted bacterial cells become coagulated and cease to function, leading to rapid cell death.

USES

Chloroxylenol is an antimicrobial used to treat cuts, bites, stings and abrasions. Chloroxylenol, or para-chloro-meta-xylenol (PCMX), is an antiseptic and disinfectant agent used for skin disinfection and surgical instruments.
ALWAYS DILUTE THE PRODUCT BEFORE USE.

8. Klindan (Glutaraldehyde 2% solution)

KLINDAN solution contains Glutaraldehyde which is a well-known sterilizing and disinfecting agent. This solution is buffered to maintain the solution at a specific pH range during use.

How do you activate glutaraldehyde?

A powdered buffer, included with the liquid disinfectant, is added to the liquid, the pH becomes alkaline and the 2 percent glutaral- dehyde is activated. Upon the addition of the powder, the container must be shaken so it will dissolve completely. The active solution has to be discarded after 14 days because of polymerization.

MECHANISM OF ACTION

A number of mechanisms have been invoked to explain the biocidal properties of glutaraldehyde. Like many other aldehydes, it reacts with amines and thiol groups, which are common functional groups in proteins. Being bi-function, it is also a potential crosslinker.

HOW IS KLINDAN SOLUTION USED IN HEALTHCARE FACILITIES?

It is used as a cold sterilizer to disinfect a variety of heat-sensitive instruments, such as endoscopes, dialysis equipment, and more. It is used as a high-level disinfectant for those surgical instruments that cannot be heat sterilized.
Contact times of at least 20 minutes (at or above 20°C) are effective for high-level disinfection. DO NOT DILUTE THE PRODUCT. CLEAN AND THEN SOAK IN KLINDAN SOLUTION IN A CONTAINER

CAUTION

Glutaraldehyde is an irritant to the skin, eyes, and respiratory system. Glutaraldehyde is an irritant to the skin, eyes, and respiratory system. Skin contact may cause minor irritation with itching and slight local redness. Prolonged contact causes mild to moderate local redness and swelling.
Glutaraldehyde may cause a sensation by inhalation.
In case of contact with eyes, rinse immediately with plenty of water and seek medical advice.
Use in well-ventilated areas.

Store below 30°C

10. Klingen solution (Hydrogen peroxide)

Klingen solution (Hydrogen peroxide) is a mild antiseptic used on the skin to prevent infection of minor cuts, scrapes, and burns.

INDICATIONS

Hydrogen peroxide is indicated to be used as a disinfectant and sterilizer.

Mechanism of Action

Hydrogen peroxide exhibits antimicrobial properties against most forms of microorganisms, including dormant forms with known high resistance profiles, such as bacterial spores and protozoal cysts. It acts as an oxidative biocide to generate free radical species to induce DNA, protein and membrane lipid damage via oxidation.
The production of free hydroxyl radicals in the Fenton reaction is thought to be the basis of the biocidal actions of hydrogen peroxide. Free radicals eventually lead to oxidative damage to proteins and membrane lipids in vivo. The oxidizing radical as the ferryl radical induces DNA oxidation.

USES

Mouthwash: In low concentrations, hydrogen peroxide can be used as a mouth rinse to remove mucus or minor mouth irritations
Wound disinfection: Hydrogen peroxide (H2O2) is a topical antiseptic used in wound cleaning which kills pathogens through oxidation bursts and local oxygen production. A proper level of Hydrogen Peroxide is considered an important requirement for normal wound healing.
This product should not be used to treat deep wounds, animal bites, or serious burns.
Do not use it in the eyes or apply it over large areas of the skin.

FIRST-AID MEASURES

Inhalation
Seek fresh air. If the victim’s breathing is difficult, administer oxygen. If breathing is absent, give artificial respiration and seek medical attention immediately.
Eye Contact
Remove contact lenses if present. Immediately flush your eyes with plenty of water for at least 15 minutes, and seek medical attention.
Skin Contact
Flush skin with plenty of water and cover irritated skin with an emollient. Remove contaminated clothing. In case of serious skin exposure, use disinfectant soap and an anti-bacterial cream and seek medical attention.
Ingestion
Do NOT induce vomiting. Loosen tight clothing. Never give anything by mouth to an unconscious person. Seek medical attention
