KLINDAN

Disinfectant

PRESENTATION

5L filled in high density plastic jerricans.

Glutaraldehyde 2% solution

KLINDAN solution contains Glutaraldehyde which is a well-known sterilizing and disinfecting agent. This solution is buffered to maintain the solution at a specific pH range during use.

HOW DO YOU ACTIVATE GLUTARALDEHYDE?

A powdered buffer, included with the liquid disinfectant, is added to the liquid, the pH becomes alkaline and the 2 percent glutaraldehyde is activated.

Upon the addition of the powder, the container must be shaken so it will dissolve completely.

The active solution has to be discarded after 14 days
because of polymerization.

MECHANISM OF ACTION

A number of mechanisms have been invoked to explain the biocidal properties of glutaraldehyde.

Like many other aldehydes, it reacts with amines and thiol groups, which are common functional groups in proteins.

Being bi-function, it is also a potential crosslinker.

HOW IS KLINDAN SOLUTION USED IN HEALTHCARE FACILITIES?

It is used as a cold sterilizer to disinfect a variety of heat-sensitive instruments, such as endoscopes, dialysis equipment, and more.

It is used as a high-level disinfectant for those surgical instruments that cannot be heat sterilized.

Contact times of at least 20 minutes (at or above 20°C) are effective for high-level disinfection.

Do not dilute the product.

Clean and then soak in klindan solution in a container.

CAUTION

Glutaraldehyde is an irritant to the skin, eyes, and respiratory system.

Glutaraldehyde is an irritant to the skin, eyes, and respiratory system.

Skin contact may cause minor irritation with itching and slight local redness.

Prolonged contact causes mild to moderate local redness and swelling.

Glutaraldehyde may cause a sensation by inhalation.

In case of contact with eyes, rinse immediately with plenty of water and seek medical advice.

Use in well-ventilated areas.

STORAGE

Store below 30°C.
